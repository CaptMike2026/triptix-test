Historical Analysis · Mid-May West End
Striped Bass Peak Window
First migratory wave staging at the Canal mouth on pogies and silversides. Keeper and trophy fish mixed. 3-week window — we're in it now.
Black Sea Bass Season Opener
Stacked on Hog Island wrecks, first weeks of pressure, aggressive on the jig. Best catch rate window of the year before pressure builds.
Tautog Spring Peak
Resident boulder fish, Mashnee complex. May is the spring window on crab. Incoming tide washes green crab off the rocky bottom — that's the trigger.
Bait Picture
Pogies dominant on outgoing. Sand eels on structure. Green crab on rocky bottom. Water 54–58°F, good clarity post-frontal.
How We're Using the Tide Today

SW wind + outgoing tide is the ideal pairing at Mass Maritime. The wind aligns with the Canal outflow and pushes bait into the seam. Bird plays form earlier. That's Phase 1.

Slack arrives at 11 AM, perfectly timed for the GFB window. Sea bass on the Hog Island wrecks go dead-drop mode when current drops. Don't be running between spots during slack. That's Phase 2.

Incoming builds at 12:21 PM and loads the Mashnee boulder complex. Green crab starts washing off the rocks. Tog come alive on early IT. That's Phase 3.

The Plan

Three Phases. One Tide Cycle.

Work every stop in a phase before calling it dead. No optimistic signs after all passes — move on. If the fish are there, you'll find them.

Phase 1 · StriperBirds working, surface activity, bass on sonar upper column, pogies visible
Phase 2 · Sea BassArches on sonar near bottom, any hits or follows, scup active on the shoal
Phase 3 · TogAny contact on the boulder face, weight on jig, crab washing off rocks

Boulder and reef face 15–30 ft on the early incoming. Green crab washing off the rocks as IT builds. Tog move onto the face when current starts loading the structure. Productive zone is tight — reset the drift often.
Approach
Bow uptide. Short drifts ACROSS the boulder face, not parallel to it. Drop jig to bottom. Short lift 6–8 inches. Reset often — the zone is narrow.
Capt. Mike's Lures
  • 2–3 oz Jig Head + Green Crab — primary presentation
  • Bucktail 2 oz — when current pushing hard along the face
Outfit
  • Hogy MH Conventional or Heavy Spinning
  • 4000–5000 reel · 30 lb braid minimum
  • 30 lb fluoro · no trailer · 3–4 ft
Technique / Retrieve
Feel the weight go heavy — that is the bite, not a strike. The moment you feel heaviness, sweep the rod hard and fast. One shot. No hesitation. Steer the fish away from structure immediately — they go straight to the rocks.
